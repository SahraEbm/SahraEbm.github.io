Research Vision

My research focuses on advancing next-generation Gallium Nitride (GaN) High Electron Mobility Transistors (HEMTs) through the development of accurate, physics-based compact models for power electronics, RF circuits, and high-voltage semiconductor technologies.

My objective is to bridge semiconductor device physics with circuit-level simulation by creating scalable compact models that accurately represent advanced GaN transistor architectures while remaining computationally efficient for SPICE and EDA tools.

Working in collaboration with the EPFL HEMT Modeling Team, my research has contributed to the development of compact modeling methodologies for advanced field-plated GaN HEMTs, providing improved predictive capability for both device designers and circuit engineers.

COMPACT MODELING

Compact Modeling of GaN HEMTs with Gate and Source Field Plates

A major outcome of my research has been the development of a comprehensive physics-based compact model for GaN HEMTs incorporating both Gate Field Plates (GFP) and Source Field Plates (SFP). The model captures the coupled electrostatic interactions between multiple field-plate structures and accurately predicts their influence on device performance.

The model includes the effects of

Electric-field redistribution
Surface potential modulation
Charge transport
Dynamic trapping and detrapping
Capacitance variation
Breakdown voltage enhancement
Self-heating
Temperature dependence
Switching losses
High-frequency operation

By integrating both GFP and SFP effects into a unified compact modeling framework, this work provides an accurate and scalable solution for the simulation of advanced GaN power devices and RF transistors.
