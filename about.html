About Me

I am a semiconductor device engineer and researcher with a strong focus on physics-based compact modeling and advanced transistor technologies.

Education

M.Sc. in Electrical Engineering — Amirkabir University of Technology

GPA: 4.0/4.0

During my graduate studies, I served as a Teaching Assistant for three terms in VLSI Design and Solid-State Electronics. These roles deepened my expertise in semiconductor device physics, charge transport mechanisms, and circuit-level analysis.

Research Focus

My recent research centers on the physics-based compact modeling of GaN HEMTs with gate field-plate structures. I developed a charge-based compact model that extends the renowned EPFL GaN HEMT framework by incorporating field-plate electrostatics and access-region resistances.

This work has been submitted to IEEE Transactions on Electron Devices and is currently under review. The model has been rigorously validated against TCAD simulations and experimental measurements.

In parallel, I am developing a 2-D analytical model of AlGaN/GaN HEMTs with field-plate structures to provide deeper insights into device electrostatics and charge transport mechanisms.

Collaborations

I am actively collaborating with imec (Belgium) on advancing GaN device modeling, contributing to cutting-edge research in high-performance wide-bandgap semiconductors for RF and power applications.
